15 years in security. Started as a programmer, stayed for the offense.

I work across the full spectrum — network, web application, red team operations, penetration testing, and custom tooling. If it can be broken, automated, or built from scratch, that's where I spend my time. Most of my work sits at the intersection of offensive security and engineering: writing tools that do the things commercial products won't, automating what shouldn't be manual, and understanding systems well enough to abuse them.

**Certifications:** CISSP · GPEN · GXPN · GWAPT · GPYC

## Faith

*"Blessed be the LORD, my rock, who trains my hands for war, and my fingers for battle." — Psalm 144:1*

I'm a Christian, and that's not incidental to the work — it's foundational to everything.

Reformed theology has shaped how I understand myself and my place in the world. The doctrine of God's sovereignty means there is no compartment of life that falls outside his lordship — not security research, not tool development, not the way I approach a target network. All of it is done *coram Deo*, before the face of God. The skills, the drive, the curiosity, the capacity to think adversarially — none of it originates with me. It's given, and it's given for a purpose beyond my own advancement.

My identity isn't rooted in what I can break or build. It's rooted in union with Christ — in the reality that I am justified not by competence or credential but by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone. That's the only foundation that doesn't shift. Every other identity collapses under pressure. This one holds.

That peace is real, and it's available — not as a feeling to be manufactured but as a gift to be received. If you're carrying the weight of knowing you've fallen short of what you were made to be, that's not something to manage around. Christ bore the full weight of that on the cross, rose in victory over it, and offers reconciliation with God to all who repent and trust in him. Not self-improvement. Not religion. A new life.

If any of that lands and you want to talk, find me.
